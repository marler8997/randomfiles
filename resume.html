My Story

I started college in 2006 majoring in Physics and Mathematics. In my sophomore year, a professor took an interest in me and invited me to research combinatorics with him. I developed programs to test our theories and spent countless hours proving them. He was impressed with my programming ability and suggested I pursue Computer Science instead of Physics. I dropped my physics major and took up computer science along with general Mathematics.

Once I started programming classes I knew I was where I belonged. Even though I was taking as many classes as I could, I wasn't getting enough work from my professor's, so I was constantly working on side projects. As I learned more, I would go back to older assignments and rewrite them with new techniques and marvel at what I could do with all this knowledge. I was learning all that I could about so many topics and loved every minute of it.

The summer after my first year in Computer Science I got an internship programming code analysis software in Lisp for one of my professors. The professor was impressed with my work and allowed me to take more classes the next semester even though I hadn't taken the prerequisites. Because of this I was able to graduate after my second year in the Comptuer Science program, along with my Mathematics degree and a 3.9 GPA.

A few months before graduating I met with a recruiter from HP. They flew me out to the site and offered me a job which I took after I graduated. I thought my education would slow down but to my suprise it was only accelerated. I wanted to learn everything! I would read RFCs to learn new protocols and create programs just because I thought I could do it better. At work I was given important projects because I was known to be able to write very good code and do it very quickly. I worked on low level drivers, operating system infrastructure, the USB stack, our build system, complex databases, interactive web sites with jquery/ASP.Net/PHP, and general .NET software.
